What is our strategy?

The Non Profit, C4D will hyper focus our efforts in 3 swing states - Wisconsin, Pennsylvania and Michigan. These states are in play for both parties and the biggest challenge to voter turnout will come from citizens unwilling to go to a polling station due to fear of their health due to the pandemic or racial unrest. The key to avoiding this is to sign up these potential voters to vote safely from home. Citizens for Democracy is working directly with organizations on the ground in these three states to sign up voters to ensure they can get their ballot in the mail and exercise their democratic right to vote.

The side that gets their voters to turn out in these states has the overwhelming advantage. And while signing up voters sound simple, voter suppression tactics have made it quite hard. Some states require a witness to countersign their request for a ballot. Others require uploading their identification to a website. These hurdles are designed to disproportionately impact underprivileged people and minorities. In addition this is the first election cycle that these three battleground states have allowed voting from home, and any new behavior requires education, help and follow up.

Vote by home strategy

In the spring, America Votes updated our strategic assumptions for the COVID-19 era and built state-by-state plans for coordinated vote-by-mail enrollment across our coalition. These plans refresh targeted universes and set goals for vote-by-mail enrollment and education of primarily voters of color and other underrepresented communities, leveraging the trust and infrastructure of our partner organizations across every stage of the effort. For this program, America Votes is ultimately targeting more than 24 million voters for vote-by-mail enrollment in the following thirteen states, including 6.4 million voters in Michigan, Pennsylvania and Wisconsin

With this target universe in mind, America Votes built a standardized vote-by-mail enrollment program treatment based on existing evidence of best practices, primarily consisting of four waves of mailed applications tied to priming and chasing communications via calls and text messages. For each wave of mailed applications, the priming window runs the week prior to the mail drop date and consists of one live call and one text message to each voter in our target universe. The chasing window runs the week following the mail drop date and consists of one live call and one text message to each voter in our target universe, who hasn't returned their application
